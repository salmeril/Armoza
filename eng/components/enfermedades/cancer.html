    Cancer

    Cancer is not a simple disease, it is somewhat more extensive characterized by an uncontrolled growth of certain cells in the body. A cancerous tumor begins with what is called a primary site.
    Cancer cells can invade and damage surrounding tissue, and then the primary tumor breaks and extends through the circulatory and lymphatic system to other parts of the body. These are the so-called secondary tumors that can also be harmful.

    Cancer can develop anywhere in the body. Among men, the most common is prostate cancer followed by lung cancer, which, although it does not happen Frequently, it is never less cause of more deaths than prostate cancer. Among women, breast cancer and lung are the most frequent. In both sexes, colorectal cancer follows lung cancer in frequency order. Other prominent types of diseases include cancers of the urinary tract, of the female reproductive system, the pancreas and the leukemia.

    The medical terms used to describe the different types of cancer are different. Most of the terms have the OMA syllable in their last letters, which means mass or tumor.
    Basic cancer categories include:

    carcinoma: skin cancer, mucous membrane, internal glands and organs are often classified as carcinomas.

    Leukemias: They are tissue cancers that produce blood cells. Because leukemia results from abnormal cell formation, this is often referred to as blood cancer.

    Lymphoma: They are cancers of lymphatic nodules and other lymphatic tissues.

    Sarcoma: They are primarily cancers of bones, muscles and connective tissue.

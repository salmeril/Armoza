    High cholesterol

    Colesterol is also called lipids, they are energy-rich substances that serve as the greatest fuel producer for the body's metabolic processes. Fats are obtained from food or formed in the body, mostly in the liver, and can be stored in fat cells for future use. Fat cells also isolate the body of the cold and help you protect it from injuries. Fats are essential components of the cell membrane, of the wrapping of the myelin that surrounds the nerve cell, and in the bile.
    Cholesterol is not quite bad;The body produces it naturally, and performs some vital tasks: it helps produce new cells, hormones and isolate nerves.It only causes problems when its amount is excessive.

    The two greatest fats in the blood are:

    • cholesterol
    • Triglycerides

    These fats break and bind to certain proteins and travel together through the bloodstream in the form of lipoproteins that are divided into:

    • VLDL: Very low density lipoproteins.
    • LDL: low density lipoproteins.
    • HDL: High density lipoproteins

    HDL cholesterol is considered good for its ability to purify arteries. The more cholesterol this type is in the blood, the better.

    LDL cholesterol accumulates in the walls of the arteries, causing their tamponade and restriction of blood circulation. Therefore how much lower the levels of this cholesterol, better.

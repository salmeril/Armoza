    Anemia

    It is the decrease in the amount of red blood cells or by the decrease in the amount of blood hemoglobin. These red or red blood cells contain hemoglobin, a protein in charge of oxygen transport. Therefore, by decreasing the amount of red blood cells there will be less oxygen supply to cells and tissues in general.As a result of this there will be less energy to perform the normal body functions; Important processes such as muscle activity, construction and cell reparation.

    Classic symptoms include:
    • Fatigue and paleness
    • Respiratory difficulty during a physical exercise
    • palpitations, dizziness, insomnia
    • headache
    • gattatrindal discomfort
    • Loss of mental sharpness
    • irregular heartbeat and even caridiac breath

    Anemia can be caused by:

    • Excessive bleeding (surgeries, menstrual bleeding, accidents, ruptures of blood vessels, gastrointestinal bleeding due to erosive ulcers or gastritis, hemorrhoids, etc.)

    High blood pressure (Hypertension)

    Arterial hypertension is known as "silent death", because it does not give symptoms until it is complicated. Being the biggest cause of death related to heart attack and spill or vascular accident.
    When the heart sends blood to the arteries, the blood pressure is expelled against the wall of the vessels in people who suffer from hypertension, the pressure is abnormally high.
    Blood pressure measures us: the systolic pressure that is the pressure exerted when the heart throbs, which forces the blood inside the vessels, this is the highest pressure.
    The other would be the diastolic pressure that is obtained when the heart rests between each palpitation, this is the lowest pressure
    The pressures are measured in millimeters of mercury (mmhg); Normal is between 120/80. Reading normal blood pressure in an adult varies from 100/60 to 140/90, while a reading from 140/90 to 160/95 indicates blood pressure at the limit. Any pressure greater than 180/115 is severe; Blood pressure can be normal, high or low.

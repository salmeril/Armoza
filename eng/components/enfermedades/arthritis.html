    Arthritis

    It is an inflammatory disease of one or more joints. Characterized by pain, swelling, deformity and/or stiffness of the joint.
    It is more common to affect the following joints: knees, fingers of the hands, fingers of the feet, dolls, elbows and shoulders. The neck, back and hip are also affected.
    There are six different types of synovial joints, even if they have different forms of movement, their underlying physiological structure is essentially the same.
    Two or more bone joints have a cartilage cover surrounded by a fluid encapsulated by ligaments on their bone surface. This fluid is secreted by the synovial membrane, which is a fine sheet located inside the joint capsule; Therefore any alteration in one of these structures can be arthritis.
    Arthritis can be of sudden or gradual appearance. Almost 80% of the population above 50 are affected, more frequently in women than in men.
    Unfortunately this is a degenerative disease that progresses progressively with age.

    There are different types of arthritis
    • Osteoarthritis.
    • Rheumatoid arthritis.
    • Spondyloartropathies (psoriatic, ankylosing spondylitis and reiter syndrome).
    • gout
    • Systemic lupus erythematosus.
    • Juvenile rheumatoid arthritis.
    • Infectious arthritis.
    • Kawasaki syndrome.

    rigidity -Deformation and/or disappearance of the normal form of the joint and adjacent bones

    If when you wake up in the morning you feel stiffness in any or some joints of your body accompanied by a lot of pain with redness and/or swelling of it, but that the pain is gradually yielding with the joint movement? Then you may think about arthritis.

    Almost all types of arthritis are of auto-inmune origin except for the following: Gotosa arthritis that is by accumulation of uric crystals in an articulation; Osteo-arthritis due to injury or defect in the protein that forms the cartilage and is more commonly related to excessive use and wear of the joint. Infectious arthritis that is caused by the infection of the joint by a microorganism either, bacteria (streptococci, staphylococci, gonococci, etc.) fungi (candida albicans) and virus.

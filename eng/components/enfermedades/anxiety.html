    Anxiety

    Anxiety is a natural response from the body in situations perceived as threatening or stressful.
    In its normal form, anxiety can be adaptive, helping a person to face challenges and preparing it for action.
    However, when anxiety becomes excessive, persistent and makes daily functioning difficult, it can be considered an anxiety disorder.

    Anxiety disorders can be physiological and/or psychological; And it can have a genetic tendency. The psychological factors that surround it correspond to the management of the autonomic nervous system.

    Some stress situations in a patient with anxiety such as: assaults, accidents or natural disasters could end up in a panic attack.

    A known reason or a subconsciously hidden trauma;as experiences of war or personal traumas of childhood, adolescence, etc. They can be a cause of anxiety.

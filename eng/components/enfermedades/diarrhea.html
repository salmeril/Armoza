    Diarrhea

    It is defined as the increase in volume, fluidity or frequency of the depositions. In healthy adults of Western society, the weight of feces oscillates between 100 and 3oo grams per day depending on the number of non-absorbable substances ingested with the diet. Diarrhea occurs when the weight of the feces exceeds 300 grams per day, except in people who follow a diet rich in plant fibers, in which case this weight is normal. Since 60 - 90% of the weight of the feces is mainly due to excess fecal water.

    Osmotic diarrhea: occurs when an excess of non -absorbable hydrosoluble solutes persists in the intestine that retain water in the light.
    This occurs with lactose intolerance and other sugars or for the use of laxatives.

    Secretory diarrhea: It occurs when the thin intestine and bulk secrete water and electrolytes instead of absorbing them. Among these we have: bacterial toxins (cholera bacteria), enteroproteus virus, bile acids, cathartic, castor oil, some drugs.

    Bad-Absorption: If unbeliered substances are abundant are abundant, hydrosoluble and osmotically important, the mechanism can be osmotic

    Exudative diarrhea: many diseases of the intestinal mucosa, for example: regional enteritis, ulcerative colitis, lymphoma and carcinoma; It causes exudative enteropathy. Inflammation, ulceration and mucosa swell

    Intestinal traffic alteration: it is due to the reduction of exposure time; They emphasize: the resection of the thin and thick intestine, gastric resection, piloroplasty and other surgical interventions.

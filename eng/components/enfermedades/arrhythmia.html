    Arrhythmia

    It is the alteration of the rhythm of heartbeat, either because it is this irregular, too slow (bradycardia) or too fast (tachycardia).
    The normal sinus rhythm originates inside the cell pacemaker of the Sino-Auricular node (SA) in the union of the upper vein and the right atrium. These cells represent the primary electric generator (pacemaker) for the normal human heart. The impulse generated here travels to the atricle-ventricular node (AV), then goes to the rest of the myocardium.
    impulse driving is a series of complicated events;All driving tissues and heart nodes contain cells capable of spontaneously depolarizing.

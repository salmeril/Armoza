    Psoriasis

    Psoriasis is a common epithelial disorder, characterized by reddened skin patches covered by silver scales.The name comes from the Greek psora which means itching.
    It is a disease in which skin cells would go apart. Usually the skin renews itself approximately every 30 days, the approximate time for a new skin cell to open its way from the most internal layer of the skin to the surface; But in psoriasis, this cell reaches up to only 3 days, as if the body had lost its brakes. The result leather protuberant areas called plaques, red and often with itching.After the cells reach the surface, they die as normal cells, but there are so many of them that the raised spots become white by the dead cells that are peeling.
    Usually psoriasis goes through cycles of eruptions and remission, although it never disappears completely, sometimes disappears for months or years.And with age it can improve or worsen.
    Psoriasis does not affect the general health, it is not contagious or spread from one part of the body to another, or from one person to another.The most affected body areas are: legs, knees, arms, elbows, scalp, ears and back.The fingertips lose their brightness and develop furrows and graves.

    The underlying cause is not known but it can result from:

    • High fat diet.
